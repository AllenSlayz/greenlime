Shigellosis: Causes and Core Symptoms

Shigellosis is caused by shigella, a type of bacteria. Symptoms of this disease can include:

  • Diarrhea
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Abdominal cramping

To prevent this, it is recommended that one wash their hands and avoid sharing food and drink with infected people.

Cell Signaling Pathway

Toll-like receptors are necessary in immune response. Activation leads to pro-inflammatory cytokines, chemokines, and co-stimulatory molecules

  • Ligand binding activates the toll-like receptors
  • NF-kb and MAPK pathways are actived
  • These pathways are mediated by TIR domain-containg cytosolic adapters like:
    • Myeloid differentiation primary-response protein-88
    • TIR domain-containing adapter protein
    • TIR domain-containing adapter-inducing IFNb
    • TRIF-related adapter molecule

Correct Mechanism

When Working correctly mechanistic target of rapamycin regulates many cellular processes.

Nuclear factor kappa-light-chain-enhancer of activated B cells (NF-kB) plays a large role in responsding to bacterial and viral antigens. It also helps the immune system respond to infection.

NF-kb

Incorrect Mechanism

Very recently in modern biological research it has been shown that Shigella inhibited mTOR signaling. This causes dephosphorylation (removal of a phosphate group by hydrolysis) of 4E-binding protein 1 and S6 kinase 1.

Shigella has also been shown to dampen the NF-kB signalling pathway. When this is incorrectly regulated it can lead to cancer, autoimmune diseases, viral infection and improper development of the immune system.

Current Direction of Research

Researchers are currently trying to develop a vaccine to prevent infection. Scientists are also developing monoclonal antibodies to fight off the adverse effects of shigellosis.

Research is also being done to observe the the virulence factors of Shigella work to cause diseases.

The end-goal is to improve the overall health of the public and prevent this disease from affecting the general population.
